Little Keepers

About:

Little Keepers is a cooperative Tower Defense game where we control Ollie and Dahlia, two siblings who must protect their home from their mother's failed experiments. To do this, they'll have to work together to gather materials and create turrets that will help defeat the unruly plants.

This game has been developed under the name of Atabey Creations, a studio composed of 37 students divided into several departments: production, marketing, 3D art, concept art, audio, design, and programming, of which I was a part.

What did I do?

  • AI Programmer: My main task in this project was to create the Single Player mode, with the AI of the second player as its core. This companion is responsible for performing the functions of a second player: collecting light materials, assisting in carrying heavy objects, and piloting the cooperative turret when needed. This AI was balanced through many iterations to be efficient but not appear overly intelligent.

    • I also programmed the protector enemy, a mushroom that grants a shield to the enemies that come into contact with it.

  • Gameplay Programmer: I was also in charge of creating different gameplay elements for the game, such as the main cable mechanic, which allows players to grab and move materials and turrets. This mechanic also includes restricted areas for planting turrets, as they should not be placed too close to each other or near key areas like the spawners.

  • UI Programmer: Since I needed to create a screen to choose between Single and Multiplayer mode, I also programmed what eventually evolved into the character selection screen, so that if only one of the two characters was chosen before the second player joined, the single-player mode would start.

  • Blueprint translation to C++: As a member of an interdisciplinary team, I was also responsible for translating some of the Blueprint prototypes designed by the Design Department to code.

    • Details:

  • Technologies used: Unreal Engine 4, Perforce, HacknPlan, Jenkins...

  • Programming language: C++

  • Available on Steam

  • Currently we are looking forward to presenting the project to the Play Station Talents Spain

    Shuvani

    About:

    Shuvani is an Isometric Single Player Roguelike where action and strategy complement each other in a unique combat system based on magic and music. Unleash your inner power and fulfill your destiny.

    I joined Raging Games during the July-September period as an AI programmer to help prepare the game for PlayStation Talents. This gave me experience in joining an ongoing project and adapting to new team dynamics.

    What did I do?

  • AI Programmer: My main task in this project was to implement the "bomber" enemy, which attacks the player by predicting their movements. This enemy also has a rotating shield, so to damage it, it's necessary to first break its shield, pass through it, or hit it through the opening it has.

    • Additionally, I also worked on improving the behavior of other existing enemies, such as the aiming of the "ranged" enemies, the attack patterns of the "melee" enemies and the movement of the "buffer" enemies.

    Nautiloose

    About:

    "In one of the most mysterious and uncharted realms of the vast ocean, where sunlight never dares to venture, lies Nautiloose Club—an enticing nocturnal haven within the Abyssal Zone.

    Dive into the enigmatic aquatic world of Nautiloose Club, embracing the role of a club manager in this beacon of untapped potential."

    This project was part of Indie Spain Jam 2023, developed in just one week. The team consisted of 2 artists, 2 musicians, 1 producer, and 4 programmers.

    What did I do?

  • Programmer: For this project, I was responsible for managing both employees and customers during the night. In the case of customers, this included handling entrance and exit to the bar, taking requests for services (such as drinks or dancing), or even escorting them out of the club. For the employees, I oversaw patrols between different areas of the bar and taking note of customer requests, providing them with their services. I also handled the movement of the fish throughout the club.

  • Other: Other tasks I took on included integrating 2D animations for some of the fish and also designing the instructions screen between the main menu and the game.

    PAWN

    About:

    PAWN is an action and adventure game developed in Unity 3D. In this game, you control Pawn, a soldier who, tired of the war between black and white pieces, decides to rebel against its own king to put an end to it.

    This game was developed as my Bachelor's Degree Final Project. The development team consisted of three computer engineering students, with me being responsible for artificial intelligence and animations.

    What did I do?

  • AI Programmer: In PAWN, I was in charge of designing and programming the behavior of all the enemies, which totaled eight: basic pawn, boss pawn, knight, boss knight, red bishop, green bishop, rook, and queen.

    • Each of these enemies had a different behavior, taking into account the concept of each piece in a medieval environment, including short-range, long-range, defensive, evasive and structural enemies, and even two bosses with different phases.

  • Gameplay Programmer: I also handled other gameplay aspects such as the player's attack, as well as some aspects of level logic, such as jumping between levels or being able to open a cage by breaking a rope.

  • UI Programmer: Within the enemy and combat section, I also took charge of creating health bars for the protagonist and the bosses, in addition to a compass that indicated the nearest enemy.

  • 3D Modeler and Animator: In order to have all the chess pieces, I had to model the knight and the rook pieces, and also animate the rest of the pieces for attack, taking damage, and dying animations, among others.

    • Details:

  • Technologies used: Unity3D, GitHub, Trello...

  • Programming language: C#

  • Available on Itch.io

    Hotel Faradise

    About:

    Hotel Faradise is a cooperative platformer game developed in Unity 2D, where we control Connor and Kate, an actor and his representative who, on their way to an audition, are forced to spend the night in a hotel due to a storm. Players must coordinate using the different abilities of each character to navigate through the various floors of the hotel.

    This game was developed in the course "2D Game Development" with three other students.

    What did I do?
